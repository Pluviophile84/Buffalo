# Asset sources and deployment notes

## Project artwork

- `buffalo-emblem.webp`, `buffalo-watermark.png`, and derived icons/social artwork were supplied in the original Buffalo website file. Confirm that the project owns or has permission to publish these assets before deployment.

## Historical imagery

- `bison-note-1901.webp`: reproduction of the 1901 United States ten-dollar Bison Note, sourced through Wikimedia Commons. The file page identifies the reproduction as public domain in the United States: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:US_$10_1901.jpg
- `buffalo-nickel-optimized.webp`: Buffalo nickel program imagery sourced from the United States Mint: https://www.usmint.gov/learn/coin-and-medal-programs/buffalo-nickel
- `gold-buffalo-optimized.webp`: American Buffalo Coin Program imagery sourced from the United States Mint: https://www.usmint.gov/coins/coin-programs/american-buffalo-coins/

Government imagery and coin designs can be subject to rules concerning misleading use, counterfeiting, and implied endorsement. Review the current U.S. Mint design-use guidance before public deployment. The site captions identify the sources and do not claim endorsement.
